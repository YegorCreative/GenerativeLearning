Who we are?

Meet the Executive Director

Generative Learning has been committed to improving teaching and learning in schools across the world for over 20 years.

Jim Roussin, Executive Director of Generative Learning, has worked as an assistant superintendent, language arts teacher, ESL coordinator, ASCD Middle East consultant, and adjunct professor.

Generative Learning’s unique approach focuses on building healthy organizations and human development through the lens of natural systems theory and complexity science.

Our Mission:

To provide leaders with powerful new lenses for seeing and influencing organizational culture toward greater wholeness, robustness, adaptivity and resiliency

Our Vision:

Unleashing collective aspirations, energy and creativity for discovering new possibilities for action, growth and sustainability in an interconnected world

Our Guiding Principles:

  1. Organizations are living systems that thrive by re-creating themselves.
  2. Mission and vision shapes organizational identity and collective work
  3. Systems thinking generates greater sustainability and innovation
  4. Human concerns are always central to any successful change
  5. Quick decisions often lead to intractable problems
  6. Diversity and differences are necessary for organizational health
  7. Rituals, celebrations and metaphors reveal and influence organizational culture
  8. Constructive conflict makes the organization smarter
  9. Knowing technical problems from adaptive challenges leads to better decision-making
  10. Effective communication is a learned practice

WHY GENERATIVITY?

Margaret Mead reminds us that if we are to achieve a richer culture…


"Rich in contrasting values, we must recognize the whole gamut of human potentialities and so weave a less arbitrary social fabric, one in which each diverse human gift will find a fitting place"

And according to Peter Senge (MIT professor)…


“Real learning gets to the heart of what it is to be human. We become able to re-create ourselves. ... But for a learning organization, “adaptive learning” must be joined by “generative learning”, learning that enhances our capacity to create"
